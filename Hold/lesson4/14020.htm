Partnership Process

CDC alone cannot protect the American people; however, by engaging with others, CDC can achieve its vision of a better world with safer, healthier people.

CDC’s funding allocations to its partners are based on mutual priorities and opportunities to work together to improve health.  As a part of the partnership process, CDC provides funds to its partners to help further its mission.

Congress appropriates money through the Department of Health and Human Services, which oversees CDC activities.  The majority of CDC’s appropriation is extramural funding, primarily going to its partners in the form of grants and cooperative agreements.

These partnerships improve and expand the scope and depth of public health services for the American people.
