Adverse Action

An Adverse Action can result in suspension for more than 14 calendar days, a reduction in grade or pay (demotion), or removal from the federal government.

This type of discipline is also documented in the employee’s permanent record and can be appealed to the MSPB, or grieved through the NGP. The employee can also file an EEOC complaint if he or she believes that the personnel action was taken on the basis of prohibited discrimination.

Bargaining Units

A Bargaining Unit is a group of employees with common interests who are represented by a labor union in their dealings with agency management.

Billet Description

A Billet Description is a listing of the major duties, responsibilities, and requirements associated with the position or job to which Commissioned Officers are assigned.

Billets

Billets are job/position designators which reflect the title and equivalent grade responsibilities held by a Commissioned Officer.

Board of Inquiry

Board reviews occur when disciplinary actions require the review of an Officer's service record by a special board appointed by the Director, OCCO, or the Surgeon General.

The Officer has a right to appear before the Board of Inquiry with or without legal representation. The Officer may also call witnesses and cross-examine the agency's witnesses.

After considering all the evidence, the Board may recommend actions including but not limited to: exoneration, reassignment, termination of the Officer's commission, involuntary retirement, or reduction in temporary and/or permanent grades. If the Officer's commission is terminated, his or her service may be characterized as honorable, under honorable conditions, or under other than honorable conditions.

Commissioned Corps Grievance

A Commissioned Corps grievance is a request by an active-duty Commissioned Officer for personal relief in a matter of concern or dissatisfaction (i.e., as working environment or working relationships with supervisors), which is subject to the control of the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) management.

Compensable Service-Connected Disability

A Compensable Service-Connected Disability is a disability received while in military service that is judged by the VA (or the military branch) to be service-connected and for which the individual is being compensated by the VA.

Competitive Sourcing

Competitive Sourcing is the process of competing for commercial activities between public and private sources, in accordance with OMB Circular A-76. MASO oversees this process in conjunction with designated competition officials.

Conciliation

Conciliation is the process of bringing disputing parties together to reach a compromise and smooth away the differences between two or more persons.

Confidential Financial Disclosure (OGE 450) Form

The Confidential Financial Disclosure (OGE 450) Form is an Office of Government Ethics (OGE) form that certain government employees and military members must complete to reveal the employee’s or member’s financial holdings.

Conflict

Conflict is not the lack of a solution, but the fact that each party has a solution and the solutions are different.

Conflict of Interest

Conflict of Interest is identified as any action or decision in which an employee is substantially involved that will have a direct and predictable effect on a financial interest of the employee, spouse, minor child, or organization in which the employee serves as an officer, trustee, partner or employee.

Delegated Examining Process

The Delegated Examining Process is an evaluation of an applicant’s knowledge, skills, and abilities (KSAs) in relation to job-related criteria.

Delegated Examining relies on an examination of candidates’ training, education, and work history to identify those candidates most likely to succeed in a specific position. Examination includes review of material provided by candidates (such as résumés) and their responses to job-specific questions.

Disciplinary Suspension

A Disciplinary Suspension is placement in non-pay and non-duty status. Such a suspension lasts for no more than 14 calendar days. If this discipline is exercised, the infraction is documented in the employee’s permanent record.

The employee can file a grievance through the Negotiated Grievance Procedure (NGP) if the employee is included in a Bargaining Unit or through the CDC Administrative Grievance Procedure if he or she is not a Bargaining Unit employee. An employee who believes that any personnel action was taken on the basis of prohibited discrimination can file a complaint with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC).

Divestiture

Divestiture as a remedy for a conflict of interest means to sell (or divest) the conflicting property. Section 1043 of the Internal Revenue Code and OGE regulations enable eligible persons to defer capital gains taxes on property that must be sold to comply with conflict of interest requirements. To defer the gains, an eligible person must obtain a "certificate of divestiture" from OGE before selling the property and must reinvest the proceeds into permitted investments.

Facilitation

Facilitation is the process of involving a third neutral party in assisting a group to work together more effectively to achieve its goals. The objective of facilitation is to reach a common ground that may be different from either party’s initial position, as well as to improve overall group relations.

Family members are parents, a spouse and their parents, children, siblings and their spouses, and any individual related by blood or affinity whose close relationship with the employee is the equivalent of a family relationship. Incentive

The interim regulations use the term "incentive" in place of "bonus" in order to distinguish these payments from those given to an individual or group to recognize and reward performance.

Mediation

Mediation is a process in which parties meet with a third-party neutral (the mediator) in an informal setting to attempt to resolve a dispute. The objective of mediation is to reach a mutually agreeable solution to the issue at hand by which a written agreement is produced. Mediators help the parties craft their own solution and do not make decisions or determine solutions.

Merit Promotion

Merit Promotion permits individuals with competitive civil service status, as well as individuals eligible for specific excepted service appointments who are granted special competitive status, the opportunity to be considered for competitively announced positions.

Merit Systems Protection Board (MSPB)

The Merit Systems Protection Board (MSPB) is an independent, quasi-judicial agency established to protect Federal merit systems against partisan political and other prohibited personnel practices and to ensure adequate protection for employees against abuses by agency management.

Minimum Qualifications

OPM establishes the qualification standards by which candidates for positions are evaluated. They detail the education, general, and specific experience necessary to qualify at each grade level of the position being filled.

Please note that we cannot establish or modify positive education requirements. This task is reserved for OPM.

Rank in Person

Rank in Person is a classification concept that bases the evaluation of a position’s relative value (the grade level) based on the combination of an individual’s accomplishments, performance and qualifications; rather than, basing this determination solely on the duties and responsibilities assigned to the position. The rank-in-person concept is fundamental to filling positions in the SES, the SBRS, and the U.S. Public Health Service Commissioned Corps.

Special Government Employee (SGE)

A SGE is a Government employee, for purposes of the conflict of interest laws is defined, in 18 U.S.C. § 202(a), as "an officer or employee . . . who is retained, designated, appointed, or employed" by the Government to perform temporary duties, with or without compensation, for not more than 130 days during any period of 365 consecutive days.

Once individuals have completed 90 days of federal service, they attain competitive status. Status candidates are then eligible for internal recruitment processes, non-competitive assignments, reassignment, promotion, transfer, reinstatement, etc.
